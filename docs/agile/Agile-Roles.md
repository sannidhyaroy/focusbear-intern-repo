# Agile Roles

## Research & Learning

### Key Roles in an Agile Team

**Developers**
Developers build, maintain, and make technical decisions about the software. In an Agile context they work iteratively, delivering working increments frequently rather than waiting for a complete product. They collaborate closely with QA to catch issues early and with the PM to understand priorities and requirements.

**QA (Quality Assurance)**
QA is responsible for verifying that what gets built actually works as intended and meets quality standards. In Agile, QA is not a final gate at the end of development, it is integrated throughout. QA engineers test iteratively alongside developers, catch regressions, document issues, and provide evidence that tasks meet acceptance criteria before they are closed.

**Product Manager (PM)**
The PM owns the product vision and roadmap. They define what gets built and why, prioritize the backlog based on user needs and business goals, and act as the bridge between the team and stakeholders. In Kanban, the PM is responsible for continuous prioritization rather than sprint planning.

**Agile Project Manager (Agile PM)**
The Agile PM facilitates the team's process rather than directing the work. They run standups, remove blockers, track progress, ensure the team is following Agile principles, and keep communication flowing. At Focus Bear, Manish Jain appears to fill this role: coordinating intern tasks, running the daily standup, and keeping the team aligned.

**Supporting Roles**
- **Stakeholders:** provide requirements, feedback, and direction from a business perspective. They are not part of the delivery team but influence what gets built and why.
- **UX Designers:** ensure the product is usable, accessible, and meets user needs. In a product like Focus Bear that serves neurodivergent users, this role carries significant ethical weight.
- **Customer Support:** act as the voice of the user within the team. They surface real user pain points, bug reports, and feature requests that inform the backlog and help the team prioritize what actually matters to users.
- **HR and Operations:** handle team management, hiring, onboarding, and intern coordination. They keep the human side of the team running smoothly.
- **Founders:** at early stage startups, the founder often overlaps with the PM role, setting product vision and remaining actively involved in day to day decisions.

### How These Roles Collaborate in Kanban vs Scrum

In **Scrum**, roles are formally defined and ceremonies are structured around them. The Product Owner manages the backlog, the Scrum Master facilitates ceremonies, and the Development Team commits to sprint goals. Collaboration happens in fixed ceremonies, like planning, standups, review, retrospective.

In **Kanban**, roles are more fluid. There are no required role titles and no fixed ceremonies. Collaboration happens continuously as work moves through the board. The standup is a lightweight daily sync rather than a ceremony tied to a sprint. Anyone can pull the next available task when capacity opens. This suits Focus Bear's small, async, intern driven team well.

## Reflection

### My Role and How It Contributes to the Agile Process

As a Mac Developer Intern I sit within the developer function of the Agile team. My role is to deliver working increments of onboarding tasks and eventually codebase contributions, keep work visible and moving on the Kanban board, communicate blockers early, and ensure what I ship meets quality standards before it is marked done.

My contribution to the Agile process is keeping my work visible and moving. That means updating task status on the Kanban board, communicating blockers in standups rather than going silent, and not holding work in progress longer than necessary. In a small team, one person's invisible blocker can stall others.

### How My Responsibilities Overlap with Other Roles

The most direct overlap currently is with **Manish as Agile PM**. He runs the daily standup, coordinates intern tasks, and is the primary point of contact for blockers and direction. As a developer, my work is most visible to him through standup updates and task status on the Kanban board.

There is also likely overlap with the **Windows Developer team** further into the internship, since Focus Bear's Mac and Windows apps share backend integrations and design decisions. Changes on one platform can affect the other, but that collaboration is still ahead of me.
