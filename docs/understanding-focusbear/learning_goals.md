# Learning Goals

## What I Hope to Learn

The most honest answer is confidence, in two specific forms.

The first is professional confidence. I have been building macOS software independently, but working within a team codebase is a different thing entirely. Navigating someone else's architecture, understanding decisions I did not make, and contributing code that others will depend on, etc. are things I have not done yet in a professional context. I want to come out of this internship being able to say that I have done them, not just that I theoretically could.

The second is about meetings and real-time communication. Standups and live calls cost me significantly more cognitive energy than async work. I do not expect that to disappear, but I am hoping to develop a clearer mental structure for how to handle real-time questions and discussions after watching how others navigate them. Even small improvements in that area would matter a lot to me.

## Skills I Am Most Excited to Develop

I am excited to work on Swift and macOS development depth. I have worked with Swift and macOS APIs (by reading Apple Developer Documentation) a bit through another project of mine, but that was self directed and solo. Working on a shipped product with real users and practical constraints will push me to understand things I have been able to avoid when working alone like edge cases, stability requirements, compatibility across macOS versions, documentation, teamwork, and code that needs to be readable to someone other than me.

By the end of this internship I want to be able to feel confident that I know Swift and macOS development, not as a claim, but as something I have actually earned through real work.

## What I Can Contribute

Probably my most useful contribution is fresh eyes combined with enough technical depth to understand what I am seeing. I identified bugs and architectural issues in the first five days of using the Focus Bear apps (macOS and Android), not because I was looking for problems, but because I was genuinely using the app and noticed things that seemed confusing or broken. That combination of new user perspective and developer understanding is something that fades quickly as you get used to a product. I want to use it while it lasts.

I also tend to be thorough and careful rather than fast. I do not ship things I do not understand, and I will try to document context that future developers will need. These are not glamorous contributions but they tend to matter in codebases that outlive any single contributor.

## One Area I Want to Improve

I want to improve on Swift and AppKit specifically. I know enough to work with them, but I do not yet feel confident in them the way I would want to before calling myself a macOS developer. The gap is not knowledge exactly, it is the kind of fluency that only comes from working through real problems in a codebase with real stakes. That is what I am hoping this internship gives me.

## What Success Looks Like

Success would probably not be like a grade or a certificate. Personally for me, it would be finishing this internship and feeling less anxious about whether I am ready for the industry than I did at the start. That means having navigated a foreign codebase, contributed something that shipped, worked through problems with a team instead of alone, and come out the other side with evidence, not just belief, that I can do this kind of work.

If I can look back at the end and point to specific things I built or fixed, and feel like I actually understood what I was doing, that would feel like success to me.
