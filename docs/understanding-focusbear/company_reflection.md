# Company Reflection

## Why Focus Bear Was Created

Focus Bear was created as an approach to tackle the daily challenges that neurodivergent people, particularly people with ADHD, ASD and autism experience. Jeremy started the mission to solve his own challenges in daily life as the symptoms were affecting both his personal and work life, mental health, relationships, and procrastination, after all of his attempts (like parental control apps) to address them failed. In desperation, he started working on a new app that would blend the best features from other apps out there, that would guide him towards better habits while reducing procrastination and decision fatigue. He based the app on research showing that practices like aerobic exercise, yoga and meditation can help with ADHD symptoms. Jeremy noted that since using the app, he has consistently done his routines, remained motivated, and took micro-breaks often that improved his lifestyle, work and relationship. Through this, he realized how underserved existing solutions (like habit formation apps and therapy/coaching programs) are for neurodivergent people, even though diagnosis of ADHD in the community is increasing ([Zablotsky, Benjamin, et al. 2019](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/31558576)), and at the same time, modern tech has increased levels of distractions with constant dings and pings (​​[Ra, Chaelin K., et al. 2018](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/30027248)). This gave him a clear reason of starting "Focus Bear Pty Ltd." as a company to guide neurodivergent users through their routines while training them to create permanent habits (even in cases when the user doesn't have access to the app), block distractions, break-down subtasks, enhance sleep quality, etc. for the benefit of the community.

## What Problem Focus Bear is Solving

The goal of Focus Bear is to be a holistic productivity solution that blocks distractions during the workday, guides users through morning and evening routines in real-time, encourage micro-breaks and gamify the experience a bit with progress streaks. The combination of (not gentle) distraction blocking and the real-time habit guidance is both unique and effective at mitigating execution dysfunction challenges.

## Why This Mission is Important

Focus Bear sees ADHD as a massive issue that affects them personally (4 of them on the team have ADHD) and the broader community. It’s estimated that reduced productivity due to ADHD costs the US economy $87-138 billion USD per year ([Doshi, et al., 2012](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/23021476)). Children with ADHD experience significantly more difficulty with study than neurotypical children ([Caci, et al. 2014](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/24355601)). Untreated ADHD also has negative impacts on relationships, finances and almost every aspect of life ([Wehmeier et al. 2010](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/20159496)).

The diagnosis of ADHD in the community is increasing ([Zablotsky, Benjamin, et al. 2019](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/31558576)) due to greater awareness of the condition and also due to the increased distraction potential of modern tech. Even people without ADHD are finding it hard to concentrate in today’s environment of constant dings and pings (​​[Ra, Chaelin K., et al. 2018](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/30027248)). The social media industry is designed on attention economy, where the product is engineered to maximize time-on-app rather than user benefit.

The specific manipulative design techniques are called dark patterns (or sometimes deceptive design). The engagement-hook specific ones have names like:
- **Variable reward loops:** unpredictable rewards keep you coming back (slot machine mechanic)
- **Infinite scroll:** removes natural stopping points
- **Notification manipulation:** triggering dopamine hits to pull you back
- **Social validation loops:** likes, streaks, leaderboards designed to create dependency

The broader philosophy behind social media's approach is sometimes called **persuasive technology** or **captology** (_computers as persuasive technology_), coined by BJ Fogg at Stanford.

This kind of design in modern tech industry decreases productivity, attention-timespan, focus, etc. and increases procrastination, doom-scrolling even among normal users. That makes neurodivergent users even more vulnerable to these dark patterns, since normal users themselves are vulnerable to it.

The entire nuance underscores the importance of Focus Bear's mission, and how traditional parental control apps are not fit for this. Focus Bear uses OpenAI integration to detect the user's focus alignment scores on a given task, and thus block the attention economy rather than participating in it.

## Do I Personally Relate to Any of the Challenges Focus Bear Aims to Solve?

Yes, I have my fair share of challenges that fall under what Focus Bear aims to solve:

- **Curiosity rabbit holes & deadline-driven productivity spikes:** I go deep and lose track of time and everything else. Deadlines make this a bit better as my brain has a time to track and anchor my work based on it. However, the productivity spikes that I get before deadlines are tremendously exhausting for my body and mind as well, which hits immediately at once the deadline is taken care of. Focus Bear encourages micro-breaks, and I can set small but meaningful deadlines on tasks/sub-tasks, which helps me focus better and remain productive.
- **Difficulty sustaining focus on low-stakes tasks:** Since my primary motivation is mostly intrinsic, driven by curiosity, I perform well on tasks that genuinely interest me. The flip side is that I struggle significantly with boring, repetitive tasks that are also low-stakes or have abstract, far-away consequences. Focus Bear's structured routine system and distraction blocking help with this by creating external structure when internal motivation is absent, which is essentially doing the scaffolding work that my brain doesn't naturally provide for tasks I don't care about.

## Someone I Know Who Might Benefit From Focus Bear

My sister likely has similar tendencies. She has difficulty sustaining focus on tasks she finds uninteresting, procrastination when stakes feel abstract or distant, and the same kind of all-or-nothing productivity pattern I recognise in myself. She manages it slightly better than I do in day to day life, but academic deadlines hit her the same way. Focus Bear's combination of structured routines, distraction blocking and micro-break reminders would probably help her stay consistent without relying purely on willpower, which is the part that tends to break down for both of us.
